Introduction

This document is the summary report for the first user survey conducted by the Grand Comics Database (GCD). The goal of this survey was to get a basic reading on who visits the GCD, why they visit, how involved they are with the community, and a very general sense of how much they like the site.

In particular, the survey aimed to give a voice to the suspected large majority of visitors who are not active on the organization's mailing lists or Facebook group.

Caveats

This survey measures visitors who felt motivated to complete a survey in English within a fairly short window during which it was open. This may over- or under-represent various populations. The language barrier is the most obvious, but for instance we are probably seeing fewer respondents who prefer other sites to the GCD simply because such visitors likely do not come here regularly.

Summary of Results

  • Nearly 70% of respondents do not follow or post to any mailing lists or social media channel.
  • Less than 10% actively post to either the lists or Facebook.
  • Most GCD visitors prefer the GCD over other sites, although a substantial minority use the GCD and other sites about equally.
  • Nearly all (98%) of our visitors are male.
  • The majority are middle-aged (between 40 and 69).
  • Nearly everyone who used the open comment box to ask for an improvement asked for something related to search.
  • Very few GCD users are only interested in recent comics, and a few comments noted the GCD's weakness in this area.

Geographic Statistics

I created several regions to prevent a lot of tiny slices while grouping things geographically. Australia and New Zealand are grouped with the one respondent each from South Korea and the Philipines who both listed English as their only language. I've labeled this the "Pacific Anglophone" region.

The other regions are what you would expect, with the three largest country populations (U.S., U.K., and Canada) standing on their own. Note that there is only one respondent in the "North Africa" region, an active indexer in Libya who indexes comics in Arabic. Unlike with the Pacific Anglophone grouping, I decided not to put him in with Europe due to the language difference. There were no other respondents from Africa or Asia.

Note: You can see the values as percentages by hovering over the pie slices.

Detailed Results

Gender

Somewhat unsurprisingly, the majority of the GCD's audience consists of middle-aged men. What is surprising is that the audience is not just majority male, but almost entirely male. This holds true in similar proportiong across nearly all significant-size user groups.

The one exception is that women make up a very slightly larger percentage of those who come to the GCD to research for writing about comics. However, the sample size is so small that one really cannot draw significant conclusions from that.
